# CREW ROLE EXPECTATIONS

**Purpose:** Define what each role on a Florida Ramp & Lift crew does, doesn't do, and is accountable for. Roles are not job titles; they are *role-on-this-job* assignments. A lead on one job may be a helper on another.

---

## 1. Roles

| Role | Typical scope |
|---|---|
| `lead` | Owns the job end-to-end. Customer-facing decisions. Safety sign-off. Final install. Submission approval. |
| `helper` | Experienced crew member. Independent component work. Material prep. Documentation. |
| `apprentice` | Learning. Supervised work only. Limited tool use. Cannot sign off on safety alone. |
| `support` | Specific job-specific role (e.g., third-person crew for complex knockdown). Defined per job. |

---

## 2. Lead — Accountabilities

### Pre-job

- Confirm the dispatch summary and any field-specific notes from admin
- Confirm crew + roles + splits with the platform record
- Confirm materials + tools + PPE before leaving the yard

### On-site

- Lead the PPE check + site walkthrough + hazard review
- Sign off on the safety record by name + time
- Make all customer-facing decisions (scope clarification, scheduling, anything unusual)
- Decide stop-work if a safety concern surfaces
- Approve any field-added scope (in consultation with admin if material)
- Capture (or delegate capture of) photos + signed completion form

### Post-job

- Review the job submission before it goes to admin
- Confirm line items + quantities match field reality
- Confirm safety record is complete
- Confirm photos + signature captured
- Communicate any incomplete / return-needed work to admin

### Never

- Skip the PPE check
- Skip the safety sign-off
- Negotiate pricing with the customer (that's WillScot / Florida Ramp admin's job)
- Modify scope materially without admin awareness

---

## 3. Helper — Accountabilities

### Pre-job

- Have the daily PPE + personal tools ready
- Review the dispatch summary
- Confirm role + split with the lead

### On-site

- Independent component work (e.g., assemble a ramp section, install handrails, secure a deck panel)
- Use power tools competently and safely
- Stage materials and clean as we go
- Capture photos as assigned by the lead
- Identify hazards proactively; tell the lead

### Post-job

- Assist with documentation
- Confirm tools and materials are accounted for before truck leaves

### Never

- Sign off on safety alone (lead's job)
- Negotiate scope or pricing with the customer
- Make a final install on a complex component without lead confirmation when the lead designated it lead-only

---

## 4. Apprentice — Accountabilities

Per `APPRENTICE_ONBOARDING.md`. Summary:

- Observe, learn, support
- PPE always
- No power tool use unsupervised in the first 2 weeks
- Cannot sign off on safety alone
- Cannot submit jobs independently until the lead approves
- Asks early, asks often
- Stops and calls the lead if anything is unclear

---

## 5. Support — Accountabilities

Used when a third (or fourth) crew member is on a complex job for a specific purpose:

- The lead defines the support role at dispatch time
- Support's scope is bounded to that role (e.g., "spotting the lift," "second-person on the heavy knockdown")
- Support follows the lead's direction
- Support's split percentage is configured at dispatch

---

## 6. Crew composition rules

| Crew size | Composition |
|---|---|
| 1-person | Solo lead — only for delivery-only or single-component jobs |
| 2-person | Lead + helper (most common) OR lead + apprentice (training jobs) |
| 3-person | Lead + helper + helper / lead + helper + apprentice / lead + helper + support (complex work) |
| 4-person | Lead + 3 supporting roles — rare; admin-approved per job |

---

## 7. Split rules

Splits depend on roles, not headcount alone:

| Composition | Default split |
|---|---|
| Lead + helper (experienced) | 60 / 40 or 70 / 30 (per pair config) |
| Lead + helper + helper | 50 / 25 / 25 or 60 / 20 / 20 (per pair config) |
| Lead + helper + apprentice | 60 / 30 / 10 (typical) |
| Lead + support (job-specific) | 70 / 30 (or per dispatch override) |
| Equal-experience pair | 50 / 50 |

Splits sum to 1.0. Per-job overrides require admin approval.

---

## 8. Communication discipline

- **Tools down at the start of any safety stop.** Lead calls back to admin within 15 minutes for triage.
- **Customer questions about pricing or scope always route to the lead.** Helpers and apprentices say: "Let me get the lead." That's the right answer.
- **Damage findings are documented before continuing.** Lead photographs, notes, escalates to admin if material.
- **Crew internal communication is on the crew's preferred channel** (phone, text, in-app once Phase 2 ships).
- **External communication with the client (WillScot)** routes through Florida Ramp & Lift admin, not directly from crew.

---

## 9. Accountability when things go wrong

| Issue | Owner |
|---|---|
| Safety incident | Lead (immediate) + Florida Ramp admin (review) |
| Quality complaint | Lead (immediate response) + admin (resolution) |
| Damage to customer property | Lead (document immediately) + admin (resolution) |
| Wrong scope installed | Lead (escalate same-day) + admin (resolution with WillScot) |
| Missing documentation (no photos, no signature) | Lead — return to site if needed; admin gates approval |
| Late submission | Lead — explain reason; admin notes pattern if it recurs |

Accountability for the role, not blame for the person. The point is to surface issues fast and fix them.
