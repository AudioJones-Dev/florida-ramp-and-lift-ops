# APPRENTICE ONBOARDING

**Purpose:** Get a new apprentice productive, safe, and integrated with Florida Ramp & Lift operations within 30 days.

---

## 1. Pre-arrival (admin / ops, 5 days before start)

- [ ] Contractor profile created in the platform (Phase 2+; Phase 1: roster spreadsheet)
- [ ] Default team pairing assigned (apprentice paired with experienced lead, typically Audio or David)
- [ ] Default payout split configured for the pairing
- [ ] PPE issued (steel-toed boots, ANSI Z87+ safety glasses, gloves)
- [ ] Tool list reviewed (apprentice provides personal tools; Florida Ramp provides job-specific tools)
- [ ] Pay schedule assignment
- [ ] First-week schedule outlined

---

## 2. Day 1 — Onboarding

### Morning

- Welcome + introduction to lead
- Review of `SAFETY_REQUIREMENTS_SOP.md` — PPE, stop-work authority, incident reporting (the three things every apprentice must know cold)
- Review of `CREW_ROLE_EXPECTATIONS.md` — what an apprentice does, doesn't do, and when to ask
- Review of `TOOL_AND_PPE_CHECKLIST.md` — what to bring every day

### Afternoon

- Truck ride-along on an easy job
- Apprentice observes, asks questions, doesn't touch tools yet
- Lead narrates: what we're doing, why, what hazards, what the customer sees
- End-of-day debrief: what was confusing, what was new, what to read tonight

---

## 3. Week 1 — Shadow + light work

### Goals

- Apprentice can complete the PPE check independently
- Apprentice can describe the job type lifecycle (pending → scheduled → in_progress → submitted → approved → invoiced → paid)
- Apprentice can name the canonical line items they've seen
- Apprentice can identify hazards from the job-specific hazard list
- Apprentice can hand tools to the lead and stage materials

### What the apprentice does

- Sets up the work area
- Moves materials
- Hands tools / hardware
- Cleans as we go
- Helps with measurements
- Photographs progress
- Writes the day's notes

### What the apprentice does NOT do (yet)

- Operate power tools unsupervised
- Make final installs (the lead does)
- Sign off on completion
- Talk pricing or scope changes with the customer
- Drive the truck unsupervised (unless licensed and admin-approved)

---

## 4. Week 2 — Supervised work

### Goals

- Apprentice can use basic power tools (drill, driver, impact) under direct lead supervision
- Apprentice can complete the safety record entry for a job
- Apprentice can submit a job report in the platform (with lead review)

### What changes

- Apprentice handles routine cuts and fastener installation under supervision
- Apprentice starts capturing photos + uploading them to the platform
- Apprentice starts entering notes
- Lead reviews submission before it goes to admin

---

## 5. Weeks 3–4 — Independent component work

### Goals

- Apprentice can independently complete defined components of a job (e.g., assembling a single ramp section, installing handrails, securing a deck panel)
- Apprentice can drive to a job site if licensed and admin-approved
- Apprentice can complete a full PPE check + safety sign-off independently
- Apprentice can submit a job report independently

### Reviews

- End of week 3: lead + admin review of apprentice progress; identify gaps
- End of week 4: 30-day evaluation; promote to full crew member if ready

---

## 6. What "ready for full crew" means

After 30 days, the apprentice should be:

- [ ] Confident with all three mandatory PPE items + when to add hard hat / hi-vis / hearing protection / respirator
- [ ] Fluent in the job lifecycle and the platform submission flow
- [ ] Able to recognize and articulate job-specific hazards before starting work
- [ ] Able to use standard install tools safely and competently
- [ ] Able to complete sub-tasks (ramp section assembly, handrail install, deck panel install) independently
- [ ] Comfortable communicating with the customer about what's being done (NOT pricing, NOT scope changes — those stay with the lead)
- [ ] Able to capture and upload required photos + signed completion forms
- [ ] Aware of when to STOP and call the lead (any safety concern, any scope mismatch, any damage, any customer dispute)

---

## 7. What the apprentice should NEVER do

- ❌ Skip the PPE check
- ❌ Suppress a safety stop because "we're running late"
- ❌ Negotiate pricing or scope changes with the customer
- ❌ Make a field modification beyond what the lead approved
- ❌ Leave the site without the PPE check + safety sign-off + photos + signature captured
- ❌ Drive without authorization
- ❌ Operate any tool they haven't been trained on by the lead
- ❌ Commit to a return-trip date with the customer (that's the lead / admin's call)

---

## 8. Apprentice-specific platform considerations

When the apprentice is on a crew:

- `crew_members[].role = "apprentice"`
- Default split percentage is lower than helper (typical: lead 70 / apprentice 30 OR lead 60 / helper 30 / apprentice 10 for 3-person crews)
- Apprentice cannot independently submit a job in Phase 2 mobile UI until the lead approves the submission
- Apprentice's safety sign-off does NOT replace the lead's safety sign-off — the lead always signs off

## 10. 30-day evaluation rubric

Lead + admin score the apprentice on:

| Category | Criteria | Score (1–5) |
|---|---|---|
| Safety | PPE discipline, hazard recognition, stop-work willingness | |
| Tool competence | Drill, driver, impact, basic measuring | |
| Install competence | Component assembly, handrail install, deck panel install | |
| Documentation | Photo capture, notes, submission discipline | |
| Customer interaction | Professional, clear, appropriate scope | |
| Communication with lead | Asks early, escalates appropriately, no silent guessing | |
| Reliability | On time, prepared, no surprises | |

**Promotion to full crew member requires 4+ in every category** + lead recommendation.
